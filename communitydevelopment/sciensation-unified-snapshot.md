## 0 — Value Proposition

**One line:** You're already thinking about lead gen, or conversion, or margins. Others running businesses are solving the same ones.

**Your situation:**
- You're running a ₹50–500Cr company. You know which growth KPIs need to move. You have views on what to do about them. You don't have peers working on the same ones.
- Nobody in your system is in the same execution trench on the same KPI. Your team executes what's decided. Your board wants the number. You're carrying the "what do I actually do about this" question alone.

**What makes the room work:**
- Interesting wrong answers score higher than correct ones. That's the rule. It means nobody in the room is performing. Nobody gives the safe answer. People say what they actually think, including "I tried this and it didn't work." That's where the real execution insight lives — and it only comes out when the room is safe enough for it.
- The session opens with the KPI on the table and a question from Tarun. Not introductions. Not a keynote. A question about the KPI that everyone in the room is already thinking about. By minute five, the room is working.

**How it runs:**
- Every Saturday. 90 minutes. 5–8 people from different industries — MDs, directors, 2nd-gen leaders, CXOs.
- 8 growth KPIs rotate as topics: lead gen, conversion, upselling, cross-selling, retention, TAT, margins, compliance. One or two each session.
- The conversation is execution only. What are you doing about this KPI, what broke, what are you trying next. Not strategy.
- Tarun moderates with questions. Not advice. The room works it out together.
- Alternating weeks: Roundtables where you work your own KPI with peers, and Socratic Dialogues where you judge DT Fellows solving a real business problem live.

**What happens in the room:**
- You're a pharma manufacturer. The person next to you exports textiles. You're both stuck on conversion. Their problem looks nothing like yours — different buyers, different cycle, different team. But four questions in, the blocker is the same: the person who owns that KPI doesn't have a defined process between first meeting and proposal. You'd never have seen that pattern thinking alone.
- That's the session. You walked in with "my conversion is stuck." You walk out with a specific structural question about your pipeline that you hadn't thought to ask.

**Heritage:**
- This format has been running since 2011. 200+ dialogues. The method was built on Socratic questioning and refined over 15 years. It started with students and got them producing thinking that surprised the professionals judging them. The room is now people running businesses.

**First session:**
- Pick the Saturday whose KPI matches what you're working on. Show up. 90 minutes. You'll know by the end whether this room is useful to you.

**End state:** You come back because the KPI you're working on next month is on the rotation. The room compounds — the people you sat with last month are now working a different KPI, and their progress on the one you discussed together is visible. Over 16 weeks you've pressure-tested all eight KPIs with people who are executing, not theorising.

## 1 — Positioning

**The word:** Scientific.

**Adjective + Noun:** Scientific Roundtable.

**Category definition:** A Scientific Roundtable is a room where people running businesses apply scientific thinking to one growth KPI at a time — break down the problem, test assumptions against what others in the room are seeing, and find the structural pattern underneath. It is not a peer forum. It is not advisory. It is not networking.

**Parent category (existing ladder):** Peer forums. The prospect knows these: YPO, EO, Vistage, TiE, industry associations. Membership-based. Broad agenda — bring any topic. The room is usually same-industry or same-stage. The format runs on experience: people share what worked for them.

**The subdivision:** Peer forums run on opinion and experience. One person's success story in one context at one time. That's anecdotal. A Scientific Roundtable runs on structure. Five people from different industries break down the same KPI. The structural pattern surfaces — not because anyone in the room is smarter, but because the method forces depth that experience-sharing doesn't reach. The insight is a byproduct of the rigour.

**Why this word:**
- The ICP already thinks scientifically. They run experiments in their labs. They validate before they scale. They built their business on rigour. "Scientific" is their native language — it's the word that makes them say "these are my people."
- No peer forum owns this word. YPO owns "peer community." Vistage owns "peer advisory." "Scientific" is unclaimed on this ladder.
- DT owns "Scientific Execution." Sciensation owns "Scientific" in the peer forum context. Same family, different ladder. The prospect who enters through the Scientific Roundtable already understands DT's language when the consulting or Hire90 conversation happens later.
- The word immediately separates this room from every opinion-based, experience-sharing, go-around-the-table format. Scientific means: we break the problem down, we question assumptions, we look for structural patterns. Not "let me tell you what worked for me."

**Repositioning the competition:** Peer forums give you a room of people at your level. You share experiences. You leave with perspective. That's useful — but it's anecdotal. One person's conversion fix in their industry, in their context, at their scale. You can't transfer it. A Scientific Roundtable puts five industries on the same KPI and breaks it down structurally. The pattern that surfaces isn't one person's experience — it's the structure underneath all five. That transfers.

**The problem Sciensation names:**
- You already think scientifically about your product — R&D, validation, experiments, rigour. On the business side, you think alone. Growth KPIs get worked through instinct, experience, and whatever your team brings to the meeting.
- The people around you aren't in the same execution trench on the same KPI. Your team executes what's decided. Your board reviews the number. Nobody sits with you and breaks down "conversion dropped 15% — where is this structurally stuck?"
- Peer forums exist but they're broad, quarterly, and experience-driven. You bring a specific KPI problem and get five stories from five people. Stories aren't structure. By the time you figure out which story applies to you, the problem has moved.

**The method (Socratic KPI Rotation):**
- 8 growth KPIs: lead gen, conversion, upselling, cross-selling, retention, TAT, margins, compliance. One or two per Saturday. 16-week rotation, then repeat.
- Socratic method — Tarun moderates with questions, not answers. "You said conversion drops at proposal stage. What happens between the first meeting and the proposal?" The room goes four questions deep. The structural blocker surfaces.
- Cross-industry by design. The pharma manufacturer and the textile exporter working the same KPI see different surface problems and the same structural fix underneath. That cross-industry pattern recognition is only possible because the method breaks things down structurally, not anecdotally.
- Interesting wrong answers score higher than correct ones. The room rewards quality of thinking, not performance. This makes it safe to say "here's what I tried and it didn't work" — which is where the real insight lives.

**The trump card — Socratic Dialogue format (Fellow showcase):**
- Any peer forum can claim "we discuss growth." With experienced business leaders, the conversation stays at the level of what they already know. The room confirms existing views more than it challenges them.
- The Socratic Dialogue proves the method is real. DT Fellows — young, AI-native — work through a real business problem live. The business leaders in the room judge their thinking on rigour, eloquence, robustness, elegance.
- When an MD sees a 24-year-old break down their industry's problem more sharply than their own VP, the method's value is undeniable. That moment can't be replicated by a keynote, a case study, or a peer conversation.
- The Dialogue alternates fortnightly with the Roundtable. The Roundtable is where you work your KPI. The Dialogue is where you see what scientific thinking looks like applied to someone else's problem.

**Relationship to DT:**
- Sciensation is the community expression of Scientific Execution. DT consults. Hire90 hires. Sciensation convenes.
- All three use the same method — Socratic, structural, scientific — and the same 8 KPIs.
- Sciensation is the entry point with the lowest commitment. No contract. No fee. Show up on Saturday, work a KPI, see if the room is useful. DT consulting and Hire90 are conversations that happen after the room delivers value. They never lead.

**End state:** The prospect hears "Sciensation" and thinks: "That's the Scientific Roundtable — one growth KPI each Saturday, people from different industries. They don't do strategy. They break the KPI down structurally. I went in stuck on conversion, heard how four other industries were hitting the same blocker, and came out with two things to change this week."

**The dinner test:** "I go to this thing on Saturdays. Scientific roundtable — one growth KPI each week, different industries. Last week was conversion. We broke it down structurally. A guy from a completely different business had the same blocker I did — same structural problem underneath. I'd never have seen it on my own. It's how I think about R&D, applied to my business numbers."

**Language anchors:**
- "Scientific Roundtable" (the category)
- "One KPI, different industries"
- "Structure, not opinion"
- "The way you think about your product, applied to your growth"
- "Stories aren't structure"
- "Interesting wrong answers score higher"

**Sacrifice (explicit):** Sciensation does not try to own "peer community" or "advisory" or "networking" or "leadership development." The position in the prospect's mind is one thing: the Scientific Roundtable where business leaders break down growth KPIs together across industries. If you want community, join YPO. If you want advice, hire an advisor. If you want to apply the same scientific thinking you use in your lab to the growth KPI you're stuck on this month — that's this room.

**Competitive lock-out:** Scientific Roundtable is what Sciensation does. When a peer forum tells you they also work on growth, ask one question: which KPI did you break down last Saturday, and what structural pattern did someone from a different industry show you? If they can't answer, they're sharing stories and calling it insight.

**Positioning hypothesis to test (Sean Ellis protocol):**
- Run 8 sessions. After each, ask attendees: "How would you describe this to a colleague?"
- If they use "scientific" or "structural" or "broke down the KPI" unprompted — the positioning has transferred.
- If they say "it's a Saturday thing where business people discuss problems" — the positioning hasn't landed.
- Track repeat attendance within 4 sessions as the growth North Star.
- The two signals that excited early interest ("execution not strategy" + "peers on same KPI") may be the activation hook that gets people in the door, with "Scientific" earning its place once they experience the method. Test whether "Scientific Roundtable" works as the invite language or whether it needs "execution + same KPI" to lead and "scientific method" to follow.
